      About Rush

      There's just one ghost town between the Mississippi River and the Rocky Mountains. And that's Rush. Fences protect the few remaining buildings. A gravel road takes visitors to the Buffalo River for fishing and canoeing. Campsites are sprinkled nearby.

      But prior to World War I, Rush was a large zinc-mining town with a population of about 5,000. The town became a ghost town in 1972 when it was included in the creation of the Buffalo River National Park System, according to the Encyclopedia of Arkansas.

      The Loving Cabin is along Clabber Creek; the next hollow over from Rush.
